Finance methodology

How I Think About Finance Problems

FORECASTING
Start with drivers, not history
I connect volume, pricing, cost structure, headcount, and timing to build forecasts that reflect how the business actually works.
VARIANCE ANALYSIS
Separate noise from signal
I identify whether a variance came from volume, price, mix, timing, labor, or supply cost, so leadership knows what to act on.
FINANCIAL MODELING
Build for decision-makers
Models need to be assumption-driven, clearly structured, and readable by non-finance leadership or they don't get used.
DASHBOARDING
Answer the question first
I design dashboards around the questions leadership asks every month, not around all the data that happens to be available.
CAPITAL PLANNING
Evaluate through risk
The return matters less than understanding what has to go right for it to be true cash flows, IRR, payback, and sensitivity.
BUSINESS PARTNERING
Translate numbers into decisions
Finance becomes a planning partner, not a reporting function, when it connects financial results to practical operational choices.
